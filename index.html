Jeremias Inchauspe is a psychologist, CONICET fellow, and junior researcher at the Center for Cognitive Neuroscience at Universidad de San Andrés.

His work focuses on computational approaches to language, cognition, and mental health. He uses methods from natural language processing, machine learning, and computational neuroscience to study digital markers of language and memory in clinical and social contexts.

He is currently involved in a research project on automated machine-learning approaches to language changes in Alzheimer's disease and frontotemporal dementia in Latin American and English-speaking populations.
