Jeremias Inchauspe is a psychologist and doctoral researcher at the Cognitive Neuroscience Center, University of San Andrés. He holds a doctoral scholarship from CONICET.

His research focuses on using speech and text to develop digital markers of cognition, with an emphasis on scalable tools for clinical assessment. His current doctoral work applies large language models to support automated cognitive scoring and dementia detection, especially in Alzheimer's disease and frontotemporal dementia.
