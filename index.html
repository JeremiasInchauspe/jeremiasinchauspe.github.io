Jeremias Inchauspe is a psychologist and doctoral researcher at the Cognitive Neuroscience Center, University of San Andrés. He holds a doctoral scholarship from CONICET.

His work focuses on computational approaches to language, cognition, and mental health. He uses methods from natural language processing, machine learning, and computational neuroscience to study digital markers of language and memory in clinical and social contexts.

He is currently involved in research on automated machine-learning approaches to language changes in Alzheimer's disease and frontotemporal dementia. His doctoral project focuses specifically on large language models for dementia detection and assessment, evaluating whether LLM-derived speech and language markers can support cognitive test scoring, classification, and severity prediction using linguistic, cognitive, and neuroimaging data.
